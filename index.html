üìã RULES

  1. Single entry keeps only Cash Book and Personal Accounts (no real/nominal accounts)
  2. Statement of Affairs is like a Balance Sheet but is not a Balance Sheet (no double-entry basis)
  3. Profit = Closing Capital + Drawings ‚àí Opening Capital ‚àí Additional Capital
  4. If closing capital > opening capital: Profit; if less: Loss
  5. For Adjusted P&L: Pass provisions for depreciation, bad debts, outstanding/prepaid items
  6. Bad debts: Debit Bad Debts A/c, Credit Debtors (reduces debtors)
  7. Provision for bad debts: Additional provision = New provision ‚àí Old provision

‚ñ∂ TYPE 3-B: Guarantee of Minimum Profit to a Partner

What it is: When partnership deed guarantees a partner a minimum share of profit.

Rule:

  1. Calculate each partner's share of profit normally
  2. If guaranteed partner's share < guaranteed minimum ‚Üí other partners make up the deficit
  3. The guaranteeing partners bear the deficit in their profit-sharing ratio

Textbook Example:

  • A's share = 48,000 √ó 3/6 = 24,000
  • B's share = 48,000 √ó 2/6 = 16,000
  • C's share = 48,000 √ó 1/6 = 8,000 (less than guarantee of 10,000)
  • Deficiency = 10,000 ‚Äì 8,000 = ‚Çπ2,000
  • A bears = 2,000 √ó 3/5 = 1,200; B bears = 2,000 √ó 2/5 = 800

‚ñ∂ TYPE 5-A: Hidden Goodwill Calculation

What it is: Goodwill not directly given; calculated from the new partner's capital contribution vs. their share.

Formula:

Total capital of new firm = New partner's capital √ó (1 / New partner's share)

Hidden Goodwill = Total capital of new firm ‚Äì Actual net assets of firm (after revaluation)

Textbook Illustration:

  • Total capital implied = 50,000 √ó 4/1 = ‚Çπ2,00,000
  • Actual existing partners' capital after revaluation = ‚Çπ1,40,000
  • Total actual capital = 1,40,000 + 50,000 = ‚Çπ1,90,000
  • Hidden Goodwill = 2,00,000 ‚Äì 1,90,000 = ‚Çπ10,000
